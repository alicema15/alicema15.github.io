Hello World

 At Archer, we start from a fundamental belief: people are inherently interested in issues of systemic and geopolitical urgency but they often lack the way to contribute their time. We believe if we empower people, through providing and building technology, they will increase access to data and engagement for themselves, their families and the world at large. This guiding principle in our work stems from our own story.

The summer of 2016 was one that rocked the Berkeley community with the passing of Tarishi Jain and Nick Leslie to acts of terrorism in Dhaka and Nice respectively. For many of us at Archer, both events changed our perspective on what global issues demanded our attention as we were either present or had friends present at both attacks. We chose to use our technological skill set to show how the product-scaling model can be leveraged for good.

Now, in its second year, Archer continues to check traditional methodology at the door. Product designers, engineers, data vis experts, journalists and investigations work side by side to invent- and reinvent how humans experience governance, and communicate and can by extension be empowered via technology.

The Archer ecosystem is built to generate actionable insight from disparate and often inaccessible datasets whether it be a press releases, bank statements, photos or government data sets. Our view of the future is one where we, as citizens, have the analytic capacity to demand transparency and promote accountability.
